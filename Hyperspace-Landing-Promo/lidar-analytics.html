LiDAR Analytics Platform for People Flow & Behavior

Converting 3D point clouds into structured intelligence about how people move through physical environments — without cameras, without compromise.

What LiDAR Analytics Is

LiDAR sensors emit laser pulses to build a three-dimensional map of their surroundings. In the context of Physical AI, LiDAR analytics converts these raw 3D point clouds into structured data about human movement and spatial utilization.

A single ceiling-mounted LiDAR sensor generates millions of points per second. The analytics layer processes this stream in real time, detecting people as anonymous clusters of points, reconstructing trajectories, and computing metrics — occupancy, dwell time, flow direction, speed, and zone transitions.

The output is spatial intelligence: structured, queryable, and immediately useful for operational decisions.

LiDAR vs. Cameras: Privacy and Accuracy

Camera systems capture visual images. Even when anonymized, the raw data contains faces and biometric features. Privacy compliance depends on software that can be misconfigured or breached.

LiDAR captures only geometry. A person appears as an anonymous cluster of 3D points. Privacy is a physical constraint of the sensor, not a software layer. This makes LiDAR analytics inherently GDPR-compliant and suitable for sensitive environments: healthcare, education, public transit, and government facilities.

On accuracy, LiDAR provides consistent performance regardless of lighting. Dim aisles, bright atriums, and nighttime operations are captured with the same precision.

Enterprise Deployment Considerations

  • Coverage planning — proper sensor placement ensures complete coverage without gaps
  • Edge processing — raw point cloud data is processed at the edge, reducing network load
  • Multi-sensor fusion — large venues merge data from all sensors into a unified spatial model
  • Commissioning — sensors must be calibrated to the venue coordinate system
  • Ongoing management — sensor health monitoring, firmware updates, and performance baselines

Hardware-Agnostic Advantages

A hardware-agnostic architecture decouples intelligence from sensing. The platform ingests point cloud data from Livox, Ouster, Velodyne, Hesai, RoboSense, or future entrants.

  • Procurement flexibility — choose sensors based on cost and performance
  • Future-proofing — upgrade hardware without replacing the platform
  • Multi-vendor deployments — different sensors for different venue types

Analytics vs. Raw Data

Enterprise users do not need to interpret point clouds. They need answers: How many people visited zone A? What is peak occupancy? How did last week's layout change affect dwell time? The analytics layer presents these through dashboards, reports, alerts, and APIs that integrate with existing BI workflows.
